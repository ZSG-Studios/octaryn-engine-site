# Octaryn Engine FAQ

## Is Octaryn Engine open source?

No. It is access-controlled and source-available to verified contributors, but proprietary.

## Can I clone it and learn from it?

Yes, if you have been granted verified contributor access. You may access it,
clone it, build it locally, test it locally, and modify it for private review
and to prepare contributions back to the official project.

## Can I fork it on GitHub?

Any private access granted to the repository does not give you permission under
the project license to redistribute or operate an independent version of
Octaryn Engine.

## Can I publish my fork, mirror, patch set, or modified copy?

No. Public or private redistribution outside the official upstream contribution
workflow is prohibited unless Zachary David Rose separately authorizes it in
writing.

## Can I ship binaries or uploads built from this repo?

No.

## Can I use the code, editor, assets, docs, or other repository materials in another project?

No.

## Can I use Octaryn Engine commercially?

No, unless Zachary David Rose separately authorizes it in writing.

## What about assets?

The same restrictions apply to assets and non-code materials.

## Do I need to complete the contributor intake form?

Yes, before your first substantive pull request.

Contributor intake page:
https://zsg-studios.github.io/octaryn-engine-site/contribute.html

## Is the form public?

The intake app link is public so contributors can access it, but the flow first
verifies the GitHub account and then stores submitted details privately in
a Google Sheet stored in Google Drive, and in the private intake backup repo
controlled by Zachary David Rose.

## What information should I put in the intake form?

Provide the information requested by the verified flow. GitHub identity comes
from the GitHub sign-in step, and the intake form then collects your legal
name, contact email, required acknowledgments, and final assent.

## Why is there a contributor assignment?

So the project owner can maintain a single rights chain for accepted
contributions instead of having mixed ownership across many contributors.

## Do I still own my PR if I complete the intake form?

Your contributions are governed by the Contributor Assignment Agreement.
Read the public assignment summary before completing the intake form, and review
the full `licences/CONTRIBUTOR_ASSIGNMENT.md` in the copied `licences/` folder
whenever you need the exact text.
